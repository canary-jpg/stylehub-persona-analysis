# High-Value Customer Win-Back Campaign Strategy

## 🚨 Executive Summary
**Value at Risk**: $1,149,037 from 555 customers **Campaign Goal**: Retain 20-30% of at-risk customers **Projected Recovery**: $230K- $345K **Investment Required**: ~$50K (email series + incentives) **ROI**: 360%-590%

## 📊 Target Segmentation

### Tier 1: Critical Priority (Top 100 customers)
* Criteria: LTV > $3000, Churn probability 50-75%
* Value: ~$350K at risk
* Examples:
    * COO3118: $4,791 spent, 122 days inactive (75% churn risk)
    * COO4929: $4,155 spent, 61 days inactive (50% churn risk)
    * COO9275: $3,718 spent, 63 days inactive (50% churn risk)

**Approach**: High-touch, personalized outreach
* Personal email from CEO/founder
* Exclusive VIP offer (30% off + free shipping)
* Phone call from account manager
* Limited edition product access

### Tier 2: High Priority (Next 200 customers)
* Criteria: LTV $2000 - $3000, Churn probability 50-75%
* Value: ~$500K at risk
* Approach: Automated but personalized
    * Personalized email series (3 emails over 2 weeks)
    * 25% off incentive
    * "We miss you" messaging
    * Show new products matching past purchases

### Tier 3: Standard Priority (Remaining 255 customers)
* Criteria: LTV < $2,000, all churn probabilities
* Value: ~$300K at risk
* Approach: Standard win-back automation
    * Generic email series
    * 15-20% discount
    * Low-cost retargeting ads

**Subject**: Your exclusive offer expires tomorrow


## 💡 Key Insights from Data

### Customer Behaviors:
1. **VIP Loyalists** (most of at-risk customers)
    * Average spent: $2,356 historically
    * Average inactive: 61-122 days
    * Action: Emphasize "missing you" + VIP status
2. **Serial Returners** (significant portion)
    * Average spent: $2,323 but high return rate
    * Action: Address product concerns, improve descriptions
    * Consider: Should we even win these back?

### Churn Patterns:
* 50% churn risk: 61-90 days inactive (most recoverable)
* 75% churn risk: 90+ days inactive (harder but still possible)
* 90% churn risk: 180+ days inactive (not worth investment)

## 🚫 Who NOT to Target
### Exclude from campaign:
* Customers with 90%+ churn probability (180+ days)
* Customers with fraud/payment issues
* Customers who explicitly unsubscribed
* Serial returners with return rate > 50%

**Why**: Low recovery probability, potention brand damage

## 🔂 Post-Campaign Strategy

### For reactivated Customers:
1. Welcome back bonus - 10% off next order
2. Loyalty program invitation - Prevent future churn
3. Feedback survey - Why did they leave? Why did they return?
4. Enhanced monitoring - Flag if they go inactive again

### For Non-Responders:
1. Final touchpoint at 90 days
2. Quarterly re-engagement with major sales only
3. Eventually sunset after 12 months total inactivity

### Learning to Apply:
* What messaging worked best?
* Which incentives drove highest conversion?
* Tier 1 vs Tier 2 vs Tier 3 effectiveness
* Optimal timing between emails
* When to give up on a customer
